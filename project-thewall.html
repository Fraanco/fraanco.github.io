The Wall.

The Wall is a “newspaper simulator,”: a digital game that challenges players to take on the role of a journalist in charge of making sense of a political event by exploring numerous documents (e.g., official declarations, interviews, letters, blog posts) to write a news article.

Role.

Creative Director, Interaction Designer and Narrative Designer

Client.

State University of Campinas (UNICAMP)

Year.

2016

See project

Context.

Objectives

The game found inspirations in games such as Papers Please and Her Story; in organizations such as Impact Games and Games for Change; and in researchers/game designers such as Ian Bogost and Nick Case .

The game’s objective was to provide an insightful experience using procedural rhetoric to instigate players to reflect on the complexities of organizing non-linear information into a journalistic narrative. Throughout the gameplay, players are exposed to multiple points of view regarding the political event they are in charge of reporting, which invites them to think about the risks of Bias in journalistic investigations.

The texts, audios, images, and videos were either created by the game design team or collected from online content archives. The use of the files is protected by the article 46 of the Brazilian Law 9610/98 and by the “fair use” doctrine.

The Gameplay.

Intro

The gameplay is organized in 3 main steps: 1. the player needs to choose a media outlet; 2. the player is presented with an email from the editor, and has some time to look through the information available; 3. The player needs to write their article, choosing the title and feature image.

Selecting the Newspaper

The user is presented with three choices of newspaper to work on, “News 24”, “Nyether +” and “La Federacio Posto”.

The videos introduce the players to the overall story: after the disappearance of a relevant political person, a wave of protests started happening at the border between two countries, San Valente and A Federação. At this point, there is some crucial information the user can understand beyond the story context: 1. There are conflicts between the two countries; 2. One of the countries is rich, while the other is miserable; 3. There is a big wall protecting the border; 4. each newspaper has a clear positioning regarding the relationship between de countries.

Collecting Information

The player is then presented to a simulation of a desktop, in which he sees an email from his chief-editor, and a series of information sources (videos, images, leaked audios, Wikipedia pages, interviews) about the case and the history of the countries and their diplomatic relationships;

Navigating through the files the player is introduced to a complex fictional World. Depending on the files and the order the player chooses to consume the information, he can be presented to multiple characters (e.g. the man who disappeared, his wife, and his son; Federação’s ministry of defense), the countries’ geopolitical history (e.g. economical development, the construction and growth of the wall dividing the countries) and even political organizations (such as the ELF - San Valente’s rebel political organization that fights against the construction of the wall).

The Article

The user has a limited time to explore the files, and once it is over it is time to organize the news article.

This step is organized in three steps:

1. First, the playerneeds to choose the Title of the article (e.g. “Hero or Villain”, or “ELF blames the border defense”);

2. Then, the image that will illustrate it;

3. Finally, the support document that will be featured at the article (e.g. the interview with the victim’s wife, the border defense report on the matter). The player can only use documents that were accessed during the gameplay.

Result

The game’s result is based on how aligned the player’s choices are with the newspaper political positioning. At this point, the user should understand the relevance of the starting video. While the first newspaper tries to be unbiased, the second is strongly pro-San Valente, and the third pro-Fundação.

Learnings.

As a conceptual development of a serious game, I consider the game a success. The systematic approach to media bias, and to the challenges of creating a narrative from a database of information was effective, and the game was featured as one of the first digital games used as support material for State University of Campinas’ History Olympics’ participants.

The experience also resulted in an article about the objectives, reference and development of the game.

From a game design perspective, however, the game needs improvements both in visual and in mechanics. For instance:

• Improvements on the user interface, making the information easier to access and read;

• Better feedbacks to the player's actions, making it easier to understand if their actions have consequences;

• Development of a consistent art direction, bringing context and personality to the navigation experience (where most of the gameplay takes place);

• Use of artificial intelligence and natural language processing to allow players to actually write their own article titles, instead of only selecting one out of three;

• Increasement on the complexity of media outlets’ political positioning, to make the game more challenging.
