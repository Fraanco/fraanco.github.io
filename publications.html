Essay \ July 2024

IA Generativa: Interações, manipulação e efeitos de escala

In this Portuguese-language essay, I explore the accelerating convergence of recommendation algorithms with generative AI and show how this fusion expands the power of a handful of dominant technology companies to shape the production, distribution and consumption of information . I warn that such concentrated control amplifies risks of manipulation, misinformation and the homogenization of cultural expression, raising urgent challenges for public policy and social communication . Finally, I argue that cultivating media-information literacy and assembling AI development teams that are diverse in gender, race and disciplinary background are vital steps to curb bias, ensure fair and representative technologies, and unlock more innovative, inclusive solutions

Essay \ June 2024

Navegando pela Desinformação: Mídias Sintéticas e Letramento Midiático

In this essay in Portuguese, I explore the critical skill of distinguishing between reality and constructed content in today's decentralized communication landscape. I argue that media literacy, advanced synthetic media detection technologies, and robust legislation are essential to counter the challenges posed by generative AI technologies like deepfakes, which can manipulate public opinion and threaten democratic institutions.

Essay \ April 2024

O Papel das Interfaces Humano-Computador para uma Ética na Inteligência Artificial

In this essay in Portuguese, I explore the growing importance of human-computer interfaces in the AI era, emphasizing how Explainable AI (XAI) can enhance ethical and safe adoption of these technologies. I discuss the significant societal impact of GenAI tools, the necessity for transparent interfaces, and the critical components of XAI—Reliability, Fairness, and Informativeness—demonstrating their role in ethical AI implementation.

Essay \ February 2015

Digital Games and Storytelling: A Study of Braid

In this essay in English, I explore how digital games utilize their intrinsic characteristics—such as rules, mechanics, interactivity, and sound design—to create unique narrative experiences unattainable in other mediums like film, television, or books. Focusing on the game Braid by Jonathan Blow, I examine the correlation between its mechanics and narrative to draw relevant conclusions on this subject.
