PK XD Gift Season Website.

I helped PK XD create a new purchase channel, allowing parents and family members to buy game currency as gifts without downloading the app. This is the story of PK XD's gifts website. It covers major strategic decisions and challenges regarding the creation and development of the website.

Role.

Interaction Designer & Creative Director

Client.

PK XD | Afterverse

Year.

2021

See project

Context

PK XD is a F2P game for kids. It is an open world, where players can customize avatars and houses, adopt pets and play minigames.

Since it is a social game, customization is very used for self-expression and storytelling, and that's why cosmetic in-app purchases are such a big part of the game experience.

Purchase Process

Buying the assets for your own account is quite easy using the game's stores and the app stores’ payment systems. However, since our players are usually kids, they can't do the purchases by themselves.

Also, you needed to be logged on into an account to make purchases.How would one buy PK XD presents?

Opportunity

We were preparing for the Christmas release, and we had an opportunity: we could make a campaign encouraging kids to ask for in-game Christmas presents, and we could make it easier for parents and other family members to purchase them!

Challenges

  • • As with almost every project in a startup environment: time was short.
  • • We didn't have the time to change in-game stores.
  • • We needed to be very careful with the items we sold on the website and with the pricing: we can't have anything sold cheaper outside of the app stores.
  • • You need to be logged into an account so you can purchase items and currency for it. Since it is a game for kids, we don't allow the exchange of items between accounts in the game. How could parents or other family members buy items as a gift?

Journey and Wireframing

Since there are two main stakeholders involved in the purchase process (the kid asking for the gift, and the adult purchasing it) and multiple touchpoints (the game, the website, email) I used a service blueprint to map the overall journey. This method allowed me to understand what was expected in each touchpoint, map out opportunities and painpoints in each one of them, and to work on the wireframe.

To avoid the need for downloads, we created a website with the items and a simple checkout that would create a gift card with a promocode that could be shared via email, whatsapp or printed and delivered physically. The player could then use our in-app or the web-based redeem feature.

To provide an easy and intuitive experience, the user flow would need:

  • •The main website with information about the purchasing options; a link to the game's official website; and a “sharing” section, making it easier for the kid to send the gifts website to whomever they wanted;

  • • A straightfoward checkout flow;

  • • The Gift Card;

  • • The confirmation email;

  • • A tutorial page explaining how to redeem your christmas gift

Results

• We managed to create a new Sales Point for PK XD gems and coins, providing the company with a new revenue source using technologies we already had.


• This new frictionless Sales Point allowed users who didn’t necessarily had a game account to purchase packs for their kids as Christmas presents


• Given its success, the tool continued to be used in later seasons.
