Deixa a Porta Aberta.

"Quem sair por último deixa a porta aberta” (Whoever leaves last leaves the door open)

Although the experiences are multiple across the letters of the acronym, there is at least one thing that every LGBTQIA+ individual goes through: the closet. Deixa a porta Aberta is a podcast that believes that life can be much better outside of the closet, and that aims to share and celebrate every coming out story.

Each episode brings the coming out story of a different LGBTQIA+ individual, written, interpreted, and narrated by the person who lived it.

Context.

By “coming out of the closet”, we mean the process of revealing and affirming one's sexuality and/or gender identity. It is a process in which the LGBTQIA+ person is subject to physical and psychological violence. According to data from the Homophobic Violence Report of the Ministry of Human Rights of Brazil even the domestic and family environment can be considered as a risk environment for LGBT people. In addition to physical violence, psychological violence and family rejection have an impact on the LGBT population, making them two to three times more likely to commit suicide than heterosexual cisgender people.

Objectives

We understand that this process of affirming a sexual orientation and/or gender identity is a process in which everyone - and not just the person who is going through it - is involved: family, close friends and school, college or work colleagues. Through this project, we believe it is possible to facilitate the process of “coming out of the closet” both for LGBTQIA+ people who are going through it, and its understanding for the other people involved.

  • Empower LGBTQIA+ people to reflect on and share their coming-out stories, expressing their feelings, thoughts, afflictions and consequences at the time.

  • Create a communication channel through which these individuals can tell their own experiences, placing themselves as narrators & protagonists of their stories. Giving visibility to narratives, creating websites, podcasts, channels and other forms of transmission.

  • Connect lgbtqia+ people who want to share their stories, artists who want to participate and the public who wants to consume

About the Project.

Visual Identity

We decided to play with the literal interpretation of “coming out of the closet” tied to the idea of “monsters in the closet” to develop our visual identity’s concept: As closeted lgbtqia+ individuals, we don't bother anyone (besides ourselves). It is when we go out that our existence is constraining, that norms are evidenced and questioned.

It is almost like we are creatures coming out of the closet.

Image production itself is also connected to the collaborative and diverse purpose of the project: the images were produced by lgbtqia+ artists invited to design creatures using whatever medium they wanted (e.g. photography, digital or traditional painting, watercolor). The creatures were then selected by the protagonist of each episode.

Social Media

Our communication strategy was mostly focused on Instagram, where we could reach most of our public. We used various types of formats (e.g. feed posts and stories, reels) not only to share the episode when they were released, but to invite the public to participate and to share facts of relevance to the LGBTQIA+ community, such as the history of Stonewall, explanations of the different letters of the Acronym, and about the differences between gender identity and sexual orientation.

Episodes

We invited lgbtqia+ individuals to share their stories through the podcast, presenting the project, its concept and objectives, and previous episodes. Upon participant's acceptance, we provided close support to the writing and recording processes - with vocal preparation and voice recording guides. The editing, mixing, and finishing steps were done in-house.

All the episodes of the podcast can be found on Spotify and other podcast tools, such as Anchor, Google Podcasts, Pocketcasts, and RadioPublic.
